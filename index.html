Threat Landscape

Northeast Mexico Cartel Overview

The Nuevo León–Tamaulipas corridor is one of the highest-risk, highest-economic-value regions along the U.S.–Mexico border. Understanding active threat actors is essential for any entity with cross-border interests.

Gulf Cartel
CDG — Tamaulipas Primary
One of Mexico's oldest criminal organizations. Dominant across Tamaulipas, controlling key border crossings including Matamoros and Reynosa. Ongoing internal fragmentation intensifies violence and creates unpredictable territorial shifts affecting U.S.-bound trade corridors.
Threat Level
Critical
Cartel del Noreste
CDN — Nuevo León / Tamaulipas
Emerged from the Zetas. Controls territory in Nuevo León and northern Tamaulipas. Known for extreme violence and military-style tactics. Critical threat for businesses in the Monterrey metro area and border zone.
Threat Level
Critical
CJNG
Jalisco — National Expansion
Mexico's most rapidly expanding criminal organization. Confirmed presence in 25+ states. Documented expansion into Nuevo León represents a closely monitored and evolving threat vector for the northeast corridor.
Threat Level
High
